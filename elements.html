January 2022 - April 2022

EXPERIMENTAL INVESTIGATION OF DIESEL- AMMONIA COMBUSTION IN AUTOMOTIVE DIESEL ENGINE

This study explores reducing carbon footprints in diesel engines by introducing ammonia, a zero-carbon fuel, as a blend with diesel using a novel surfactant-based method (Span 80). Testing on a twin-cylinder engine revealed a 5% increase in brake thermal efficiency and a 40% reduction in HC and CO emissions at low and mid-load conditions. While NOx emissions increased, further analysis could mitigate this, demonstrating ammonia's potential to enhance efficiency and reduce emissions in internal combustion engines.
